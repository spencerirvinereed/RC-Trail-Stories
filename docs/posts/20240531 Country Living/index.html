Poverty with security is better than plenty in the midst of fear and uncertainty

One day, the city mouse went to the countryside to visit the country mouse. Seeing the country mouse’s meager food, the city mouse felt sorry for him and invited the country mouse to the city, promising that there were plenty of delicious foods to eat there. The city mouse took the country mouse to a kitchen full of food and told him to eat as much as he wanted. Although the country mouse was envious of the city mouse and tried to eat the food, he couldn’t because people kept coming in, forcing him to run away each time. Unable to eat, he only ended up hungrier. The country mouse then said to the city mouse, “No matter how much delicious food there is here, it’s better to live in the countryside, where I can live peacefully, even if the food is humble.” With that, he returned to the countryside. Depending on the version, the setting can appear as a field and a granary instead of the city and countryside.

This is a story that most Koreans know, originally an Aesop’s Fable.
